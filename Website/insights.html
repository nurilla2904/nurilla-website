New Investment Framework: Key Changes for Foreign Investors

March 2026

Uzbekistan's updated Investment Law introduces revised investor guarantees, new dispute resolution provisions, and sector-specific incentive structures taking effect Q2 2026.

Investment High Priority

What Changed

The revised Investment Law (effective 1 April 2026) introduces three substantive changes that directly affect foreign investors operating or planning to enter Uzbekistan:

  • Investor Guarantees: Enhanced protections against nationalisation and expropriation, now aligned with OECD standards. Compensation timelines reduced from 60 to 30 days.
  • Dispute Resolution: Foreign investors may now choose international arbitration (ICSID or Stockholm) without first exhausting local remedies — a significant departure from the prior framework.
  • Sector Incentives: New incentive tiers for green energy, pharmaceutical manufacturing, and logistics. Tax holidays extended to 7 years in priority zones.

What This Means for You

Companies with existing investment agreements should review whether renegotiation to capture the new guarantee provisions is advantageous. New market entrants benefit most from the streamlined arbitration pathway. Sector-specific eligibility criteria require review against your operational scope.

WTO Accession Update: Where Uzbekistan Stands

February 2026

Following the latest round of bilateral negotiations, Uzbekistan has advanced tariff and services commitments. This brief outlines outstanding issues, expected timeline, and practical implications for retail, logistics, and digital operators.

Trade Policy WTO

Current Status

Uzbekistan's WTO Working Party met for its 8th session in Geneva in January 2026. Significant progress was recorded on goods tariff schedules, with average MFN rates expected to settle near 9.5% — down from the current 14.2%.

Key Outstanding Issues

  • Services schedule — particularly financial services and telecommunications — remains contested with the EU and US delegations.
  • SPS/TBT alignment with WTO standards is on track but requires additional legislative changes expected by mid-2026.
  • State trading enterprises and subsidy notifications are under scrutiny.

Projected Timeline

Accession is now projected for late 2027 at the earliest. For companies in retail, logistics, and digital services, this opens planning windows for market entry structuring and licensing strategy ahead of the transition.

Digital Economy Regulation: What's Coming in 2026

January 2026

Uzbekistan is preparing a comprehensive digital economy law covering data localisation, e-commerce licensing, and platform liability. Key provisions expected to affect tech, fintech, and logistics operators.

Digital Regulation High Priority

Legislative Pipeline

The draft Digital Economy Development Law is expected to pass parliament by Q3 2026. Based on current drafts circulated to stakeholders, three provisions are of particular significance to international operators:

  • Data Localisation: Personal data of Uzbek citizens must be stored on servers within Uzbekistan. Transition period of 18 months from enactment.
  • E-Commerce Licensing: Platforms generating over UZS 10bn annually will require a digital commerce licence. Application process and fee structure still under consultation.
  • Platform Liability: Marketplace platforms face new duties of care for third-party seller compliance — similar to EU DSA provisions but with broader scope.

Who Is Affected

Any company operating a digital platform, marketplace, payment gateway, or data-driven service in Uzbekistan should begin compliance planning now. The 18-month transition for data localisation is shorter than it appears given infrastructure procurement lead times.

Regulatory Alert

Currency Regulation Update: New Rules for Foreign Exchange Transactions

January 2026

The Central Bank of Uzbekistan has issued updated foreign exchange regulations affecting repatriation of profits, intercompany loan arrangements, and documentation requirements for capital transfers.

Finance FX

What Has Changed

Effective 1 February 2026, the Central Bank's updated Resolution No. 47 introduces the following changes:

  • Profit Repatriation: Documentation requirements simplified — single notarised certificate of tax compliance replaces the prior three-document package.
  • Intercompany Loans: Loans from foreign parent companies now require CBU registration only above USD 500,000 (previously USD 100,000).
  • Capital Transfer Timelines: Processing time for approved transfers reduced from 10 to 5 business days.

Practical Impact

These changes reduce administrative burden for most multinational structures. However, companies with complex holding structures or multiple currency exposures should verify that their current banking mandates are aligned with the new documentation standards.
